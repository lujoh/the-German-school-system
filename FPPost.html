A Selection of Continuing Education Options

Berufsschule im dualen System (dual system apprenticeship)
-This is an education option that combines practical job experience with theoretical education. Students spend half their time working in an apprenticeship at a business in their field and the other half studying at a Berufsschule (job school) which teaches the theoretical knowledge of the chosen job in addition to general classes such as math, German, politics and sometimes religion. The Dual Apprenticeship is generally a paid position (1).
Berufsfachschule (vocational school)
-This option teaches both practical and theoretical information at the school and they often involve multiple internships.
Fachoberschule (FOS) (technical school)
-This system allows students to pick a concentration from the subjects, economics and administration, technology, health and social, design, nutrition and housekeeping, agriculture-, organic- and environment technology. The FOS is designed to be done after completing tenth grade and will lead to a subject specific Abitur (Fachabitur)(2).
Berufsoberschule(BOS)(higher vocational school)
-This option is similar to the FOS only that it is intended for students who have already completed an apprenticeship or and education at the Befufsfachschule.
University
-Universities offer a wide variety of academic degrees, similar to US Universities. Admission to certain degrees is very competitive and is based on the grade average in the Abitur, years of experience in the field and waiting time. Public Universities are tuition free, but sometimes materials such as books do need to be bought. There is financial aid and scholarships available for students to cover living expenses.
Fachhochschule (University of applied sciences)
-This option is similar to Universities, but the degrees are more practically focused than academically
