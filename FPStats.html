Parental Education Levels

The parental level of education is one of the highest indicators for which school a student will attend after grade school. The chart on the left shows the correlation in the year 2015 between the highest degree the parents earned and the school the children went on to attend. Children whose parents had a high level of education went on to attend the Gymnasium in 61% of cases compared to 14% in the lower level of education category. On the other hand, those with the lower level of education were far more likely to attend the Hauptschule at 22%, which was only at 3% for the higher education category.

Childcare Percentages

Early childcare in the form of Kindergarten (ages 3-6) and Kinderkrippe (under 3 years) is very beneficial to children. In groups with other children they learn essential social skills. A variety of activities, both free and lead by teachers, help children improve their motor skills.
